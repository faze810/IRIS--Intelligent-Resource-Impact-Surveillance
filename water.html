What Degrades Groundwater Quality?
🏭
Industrial Effluents
Factories discharge heavy metals and chemicals into soil, contaminating aquifers — especially in industrial corridors of Maharashtra and Gujarat.
🌾
Agricultural Runoff
Pesticides, nitrates and fertilisers leach into groundwater. Excess irrigation raises TDS levels in arid states like Rajasthan and Punjab.
🧱
Geological Leaching
Natural arsenic (Gangetic belt) and fluoride (peninsular India) dissolve into groundwater from rock formations — no human cause involved.

Health Impacts of Poor Water Quality
🦷
Fluorosis
Excess fluoride causes mottled teeth and weakened bones. Endemic in 20+ Indian states. Skeletal fluorosis is irreversible.
☣️
Arsenicosis
Long-term arsenic ingestion causes skin lesions, peripheral neuropathy, and multiple organ cancers. Affects millions in the Gangetic plains.
🦠
Waterborne Diseases
High TDS correlates with microbial contamination — leading to cholera, typhoid and diarrhoeal disease, causing ~400,000 deaths/year in India.
